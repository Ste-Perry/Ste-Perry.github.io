What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

I enjoy thinking out loud and find talking through each step of a problem. When frustrated it is good strategy to think “what question do I need to ask to solve this problem“. I find myself I can take my time and think of the task ahead instead of diving in and dealing with it when a problem when it arises.

What are the role of values, empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Empathy is a particularly important skill to have when working with other people, being able to understand how somebody else is learning and how they are feeling has a direct result of how we communicate and ultimately getting things done as a team. We are only human after all
Having self-awareness of how you are feeling is important. Sometimes you will be working with a person or a project that you do not enjoy working with and being conscious of that fact can have a huge impact, just having the awareness allows you to the ability to choose how you respond, rather then just reacting to what you are given.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

The thing that has surprised me the most about core principles is how much it around in everyday jobs and life. Most of the core learning I knew already just from my personal experience in the workplace. I belive this should be tought at school to prepare everyone for there 1st job.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Writing the blogs was the most challenging for me. I knew what I wanted to say but finding the words to put down was hard, 'was it enough?', 'did i get my point across?' ETC. I was always changing my mine and knowing that everyone else would read it was a bit daunting.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

It would make sense for any industry training organization to focus heavily on core skills, this is because 99% of all jobs require human to human interactions. For a company or team to be successful everybody in the team must be able to work together and move in the direction towards a common goal.
The reason I think EDA as a programming school focuses on human skill so heavily is because the essence of a programmer’s job is to solve problems. When solving problems in a team it is extremely easy for people to have differing ideas and ways of going about solving said problem. Frustrating can lead to a whole bunch of negative emotions and it is critical that we understand this and have tools to communicate and manage our emotions in a productive way.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

Focusing on core skills will never be a “waste of time”. I have learnt from previous jobs that human skills and your reputation within a company has a much greater weighting on your success then your technical abilities but having the skills to go along with it helps a lot too.
