Work vs Family

A biggest ethical decision I have every made was leaving my job. I was running a sports bar/night club at the time and I built help build it from scratch and built a great team. I had been working for the same people for 7 years, they looked after me, payed me well and gave me all the time off I needed to go away to see my son every 5 weeks. Until Ryan (my son) and his mum decided to move back Wellington.

This was my chance to see my son more, but my job was full on. And with Ryan turning 5 and school starting with me working nights it was always in the back of my mind I would hardly see him as much as I wanted too. Do I give up the job I have been working so hard for or do I give up seeing Ryan more?

I tried to make both work as much as I could, but this led me to losing a lot of sleep and wasn’t as motivated to be at work or wasn’t the type of dad I normally am with Ryan. This got me thinking a lot. My mum always told me I was a good dad, and I would do anything for Ryan. I worked my ass off for money so Ryan could have a decent life, but would it be a decent life if he hardly sees me. So, I took the plunge. I could always find another more job suitable. I gave my boss 3 months’ notice, trained up my replacement and went job hunting. Even though this was hard for my bosses to take in they understood.

I found a job easy, but the pay was not as good as I expected but I was spending more time with Ryan. I was a lot happier, I could be there for Ryan a lot more and spend time with him and I feel he was a happier too.

Strengths and Limitations

Throughout of my research a few words jumped out to me. Hardworking, competitive, understanding/approachable. With each of these words I thought about how dose this relate to me. Each word I would associate with both my strengths and/or limitations. I feel my work ethic would be one of my best strength to help me in my learning journey I always try to work to the best of my ability but also I sometimes work too much.

A sitiation ith working in hospitality a lot of the work and been in a team. One of my favourite things was to train new people but with workload getting a bit big, so I handed over training to another manager. After a few weeks I found out the manager I handed over too was not training anyone properly and expected everyone to know what to do after being shown once. This effected the whole business not just me.

I had to teach this manager again on how to train someone properly and she wasn’t happy about it. She is a bit older than me and didn’t like being told how to do her job. I reminded her about the day she started and how I trained her for the whole week. She came around and understood what I was talking about. I believe we could have not had this conflict if I went over training with her before handing it over. Yes she was confident to take this task but just needed a recap on how and what to do.
